Background Info


Rapper and singer Jamell Demons, also known as YNW Melly, has had a successful career despite facing serious legal problems throughout his career.

Early Life:

YNW Melly was raised in a community known for high crime rates and financial difficulties. He was born in Gifford, Florida, on May 1, 1999. Despite challenges, Melly became interested in music at an early age. He started writing and recording songs when he was younger, using his own struggles and experiences as inspiration. SoundCloud served as his creative source, giving him the chance to share his early work with an expanding audience as the SoundCloud rap community began to take note of him.

YNW Melly Rise:

As he improved, Melly's musical career really took off in the middle of the 2010s. His fan base was captivated by  songs he released, such as "Virtual (Blue Balenciagas)", "Catching Feelings"and "Murder on My Mind." In addition to receiving positive reviews, "Murder on My Mind" became financially successful as well. It amassed millions of streams and caught the interest of listeners.
2018 saw the release of YNW Melly's first studio album, "I Am You." One of the album's best songs was "Mixed Personalities," a collaboration with Kanye West. His place in the rap and hip-hop scenes became stronger with this album. A newer generation of listeners enjoyed Melly's music because of its honest lyrics and melodic flow.

Legal Issues:

With his rapid rise in the music industry, YNW Melly's career took a drastic and  turn in the early months of 2019. In February , he was taken into custody and charged with two charges of first-degree murder. These accusations came from his alleged involvement in the deaths of two close friends and members of the YNW rap group, Anthony Williams (YNW Sakchaser) and Christopher Thomas Jr. (YNW Juvy). Throughout the legal proceedings, YNW Melly claimed innocence which caused a number of issues court system as well as hip-hop culture. He was able to write songs while imprisoned and raised public awareness of his situation. His musical career was clouded by the legal issues, creating doubt on his future.

Throughout this case, the state has demonstrated irresponsibility, as the main detective was found to have not done his job properly, and the state has engaged in illegal activities such as looking for Calby without a warrant and withholding material from the defense team. They were also found to be lying about the search warrant, which resulted in the removal of the primary prosecutor from the trial and the removal of the lead detective testimony.
